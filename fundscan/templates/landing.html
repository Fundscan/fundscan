Due diligence

The questions worth asking.

How does this differ from free rate pages such as CoinGlass?+

Free services publish gross rates. FundScan publishes what survives execution: yield net of all four legs and slippage, with breakeven cycles stated on every instrument. A gross rate without its costs is an incomplete figure — and incomplete figures are how apparent opportunities become realised losses.

Are exchange API keys required?+

No — at no point. FundScan reads public market data exclusively. There is no account connection, no credential storage, and no mechanism by which the service could access client funds, even in principle.

Does the service execute trades?+

No, by design. FundScan is an analytics service, not an execution venue or managed strategy. All positions are opened and closed by you, on your own accounts, under your own judgement. This division keeps you in control and keeps our incentives clean.

An instrument shows an extreme yield. Is it genuine?+

Genuine as printed; rarely achievable at size. Extreme rates concentrate in illiquid instruments where depth and slippage consume the apparent edge. FundScan flags statistical outliers rather than featuring them. A rate that cannot be traded at reasonable size is not an opportunity — it is an advertisement.

How is data accuracy assured?+

By invitation to verify. The complimentary tier exists so any figure can be compared against the venue's own published funding page. If our figures do not reconcile with the source, we have failed at the only task we have.

Will subscribers profit?+

No such assurance is made — and none should be accepted from anyone. Funding strategies carry genuine risk: rates invert, executions slip, and conditions can remain unfavourable for extended periods. FundScan provides accurate figures on which to base decisions. The decisions, and their outcomes, remain yours.
